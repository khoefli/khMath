Welcome to this web-page!

These pages serve as a review on the theory of linear operators acting in complex Banach or Hilbert spaces and their spectral properties. The subject itself belongs to the mathematical area of functional analysis and has many useful applications, especially to linear partial differential operators of elliptic and parabolic type and their associated boundary and initial value problems.

By use of spectral theory one can study resonance phenomena appearing in mathematical physics and engineering science. Here we are led to so-called eigenvalue problems, that is, equations of the form \(A u = \lambda u\) with \(A\) being a linear-elliptic partial differential operator, where the question arises, for which complex parameters \(\lambda \in \mathbb{C}\) this equation has non-trivial solutions. But spectral theory also forms the foundation for semigroup theory, which is a decicional tool to solve evolution equations of the form \(\dot{u} + A u = f\) and \(\ddot{u} + A u = f\), respectively.

I wrote this treatise in honour of Prof. Dr. P. Werner (1932-2018), who conveyed my fascination of these beautiful mathematical topics by his lessons hold in 1984 and 1985 at Mathematical Institute A of University Stuttgart.

    T. Kato: Perturbation Theory for Linear Operators

    This is Kato's main book, first published in 1967. Rather its title contains the phrase 'perturbation theory', it covers many topics from classical functional analysis. But the main subjects are spectral and semigroup theory. Chapter VI gives a good account on sesquilinear forms and their relationships to linear operators.

      10. Perturbation of continuous spectra and unitary equivalence

            M. Reed & B. Simon: Methods of Modern Mathematical Physics I-IV

            This is a four-volume work by Michael Reed and Barry Simon done in the eigthies of the last century. Especially the first volume is nearly pure mathematics and seems to be one of the best functional analysis text books. But the other volumes are of interest for mathematicians as well, since they offer applications of functional analysis to problems in quantum mechanics.
